# Community Code of Conduct

## COMMUNITY

We want a productive, supportive, and agile community where people can share ideas, learn from each other, and collaborate effectively. Data science projects can be complex and messy, and this Code of Conduct exists so we can work through that complexity with respect, professionalism, and care.

We gain strength from diversity and actively welcome participation from people with different backgrounds, experiences, identities, and perspectives. We will challenge prejudice and harmful behavior that could limit anyone’s ability to contribute.

This Code of Conduct applies to everyone participating in this repository and related project spaces. It governs how we behave in public or private whenever the project may be judged by our actions. We expect it to be honored by anyone who:
- contributes code, writing, reviews, or feedback,
- participates in discussions or issue threads,
- represents the project officially or informally, or
- claims affiliation with this project.

### We strive to:

**Be considerate**  
Our work will be used by others, and we also depend on the work of teammates and contributors. Decisions and communication should consider how they affect others.

**Be respectful**  
Disagreement is normal, but disrespect is not. We assume good intent, communicate clearly, and avoid personal attacks. A community where people feel threatened or unwelcome is not productive.

**Take responsibility for our words and actions**  
We all make mistakes. When we do, we own them. If someone has been harmed or offended, we listen carefully and work to repair the situation.

**Be collaborative**  
This project is a shared outcome made by multiple people. We coordinate work to reduce redundancy, keep progress visible, and support each other through reviews and feedback. We prefer to work transparently and involve others early.

**Be generous**  
We share credit, time, and knowledge. We aim to help others succeed (especially new contributors) and keep communication constructive.

**Value clarity and constructive resolution**  
Disagreements (social or technical) happen. We do not allow them to linger in a way that creates uncertainty. We work to resolve conflict constructively and document decisions when needed.

**Ask for help when unsure**  
No one is expected to know everything. Questions are encouraged—asking early prevents bigger issues later. Those who are asked should respond with patience and helpfulness.

**Step down considerately**  
If someone disengages, we ask that they do so in a way that minimizes disruption (e.g., communicating early, handing off work, and documenting progress where possible).

---

## EXPECTED BEHAVIOR

Examples of positive behavior include:
- Respectful communication and active listening
- Constructive feedback focused on the work, not the person
- Using welcoming, inclusive language
- Being mindful of tone in text-based communication
- Supporting equitable workload distribution and fair collaboration
- Crediting others for their work and ideas

---

## UNACCEPTABLE BEHAVIOR

Unacceptable behavior includes:
- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Derogatory comments based on identity or protected characteristics
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted sexual attention
- Trolling, insulting remarks, or deliberate disruption of discussions
- Personal attacks, doxxing, or sharing private information without consent
- Any conduct that a reasonable person would consider threatening or hostile

---

## REPORTING AND ENFORCEMENT

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any concerns:
- **For this course project:** report to the project maintainers (the team) via a GitHub Issue labeled `conduct` (if safe), or contact the maintainers privately (e.g., via course communication channels agreed by the team).
- If the situation involves safety concerns or serious misconduct, escalate to the appropriate course instructional staff.

All reports will be handled respectfully and as promptly as possible. We will maintain confidentiality to the extent possible and appropriate.

### Enforcement actions may include:
- A private warning and request to stop the behavior
- A request for an apology or corrective action
- Temporary restriction from participation (issues/PRs)
- Removal from the project spaces (where possible)
- Escalation to course staff if needed

---

## SCOPE

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including:
- GitHub issues, pull requests, reviews, and discussions
- any collaboration spaces used to coordinate the project
- in-person or online meetings when representing the project
